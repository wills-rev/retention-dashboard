Coverage Strategy

Protect Retention With Focused Client Coverage

Coverage should prioritize the client segments where proactive engagement most directly protects retained revenue, migration success, and production stability.

Tigerpaw > $1k MRRFocused coverage for higher-value Tigerpaw accounts to protect retention and identify migration or product-risk signals early.
Legacy TBS conversionsDedicated attention for clients converting off TBS so transition risk is visible, owned, and resolved before it becomes churn risk.
Billing / Odin > $10k MRRQuarterly CBRs for accounts above $10k MRR; accounts above $20k MRR will be offered and completed onsite visits at least 1–2x per year.
Web post-graduationProduction coverage after graduation so Web clients have a clear support and success path for any live-operating needs.

01
Goals And Lessons Learned

Lessons Learned

The right question moved from "how do we sell more?" to "how do we sell more of what we can implement, retain, and turn into fans?"

Working Copy

  • Bookings are not the finish line.
  • Product activity is not automatically revenue strategy.
  • Missing joins are operating defects, not reporting inconvenience.
  • ICP must become operational, not aspirational.
  • Cross-functional decisions need one lifecycle view.

Visual Direction

  • Five short lesson cards around one center phrase: Promise -> Proof.
02
Start / Stop / Continue

Start

Start focusing the system around the right buyers, the right message, and Revay as a client-facing advantage.

Actuals

  • Focusing go-to-market efforts on a new definition of qualified buyer.
  • New messaging that aligns with who we are.
  • Expecting Revay to be on par with internal agents and evangelizing it with clients.
03
Start / Stop / Continue

Stop

Stop tolerating short-term behavior and disconnected systems that weaken execution.

Actuals

  • Chasing the number / short-term mindset.
  • System issues with no logging or proactive communication.
  • Disjointed functionality that does not flow together in a cohesive end-to-end workflow.
04
Start / Stop / Continue

Continue

Continue the foundations that still matter: mission, channel focus, and close client relationships.

Actuals

  • Building toward our mission and vision.
  • Push into the IT channel with established providers.
  • Intimate relationships with clients.

ICP Is A Fit System, Not A Persona

ICP should define where Rev.io can win, implement, retain, and create advocates now.

Working Copy

  • Win with controlled roadmap commitment.

Direct sales should move from broader opportunity pursuit to higher-quality retained-revenue creation.

Working Copy

  • Separate not now from not relevant.
  • Tighten handoff into implementation readiness.

  • Sales motion flow: Trigger -> Fit -> Proof -> Commitment -> Readiness -> Hand-off.
